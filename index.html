About

I am a marine ecologist with a fascination for the interplay between humanity and nature. My research interest lies at the interface between natural resource management and the human dimension of fisheries, particularly focusing on small-scale fisheries and the use of co-management in ocean governance.

As part of the Humboldt Tipping project at the Center for Tropical Marine Research (ZMT) in Bremen, I am currently working on a social-ecological network model of coastal benthic habitats and their users within the Humboldt Upwelling System in Peru.

teaching

Beyond publications

Communicating science to where it can create the largest impact is my passion. I am dedicated to introducing the younger generation to the fascinating world of marine ecology and have diverse experience working with kids from kindergarten to scholars at university level. I teach in both classroom as well as outdoor settings: from national park to lecture hall; from land-based workshops to high school education on the open-ocean.

About

I am a marine ecologist with a fascination for the interplay between humanity and nature. My research interest lies at the interface between natural resource management and the human dimension of fisheries.

As part of the Humboldt Tipping project at the Centre for Tropical Marine Research (ZMT) in Bremen, I am currently working on a social-ecological network model of coastal benthic habitats and their users within the Humboldt Upwelling System in Peru.

I recently joined the Thuenen Institute in Rostock, working on fisheries management issues within the marine protected areas of the Baltic Sea. As part of the newly launched research mission "Protection and Sustainable Use of the Ocean" of the German Marine Research Alliance (DAM), I investigate the effects of exclusion of certain types of fishing within marine protected areas on the local fish fauna.

teaching

Beyond publications

Communicating science to where it can create the largest impact is my passion. I am dedicated to introducing the younger generation to the fascinating world of marine ecology and have diverse experience working with kids from kindergarten to scholars at university level. I teach in both classroom as well as outdoor settings: from national park to lecture hall; from land-based workshops to high school education on the open-ocean.
